Introduction of RBX Technology

RBX Technology can detect and display vascular structures and high concentrations of melanin.

It provides a more refined visualization of sub-surface melanin and vascular conditions for all skin types and ethnicities.

RBX Color Space

RBX technology can represent skin images in terms of melanin and hemoglobin components.

Insofar that primary color signatures Red and Brown denote hemoglobin and melanin components respectively, this color space is called Red/Brown/X (RBX). RBX transforms an RGB image in a camera into the RBX color-space where the Red and Brown channels represent hemoglobin and melanin distributions, respectively.

The RBX color-space design is based on facial skin images of patients with different skin types. This color model is based on the light transport model of skin as illustrated in Figure 2. According to this model, melanin and hemoglobin are the dominant chromophores affecting the remitted light (i.e., skin color).

For accurate imaging of melanin and hemoglobin, it is essential that the re-emitted light from the skin is free of specular reflections (Figure 2). The skin image is captured under polarized illumination with a pair of orthogonally-polarized filters placed over the flash and on the camera lens respectively. Cross-polarization eliminates specular reflections from the skin surface, improving visibility of re-emitted light from the epidermis and dermis where melanin and hemoglobin reside (Figure 3).

RBX Technology Applications

Figure 4

1. Capture cross-polarized images by various Canfield imaging systems.

2. Apply RBX process and display Red/Brown images.

3. Red/Brown images can be further processed to identify and highlight hyper-pigmented spots and vascular structures.

4. Generate a severity score associated with the degree of pigmentation and a severity score associated with the vascular structures. The severity scores provide quantitative measures for hyper-pigmentation and vascularization. They can be used to rate patients and monitor the progress after treatment.

Figure 5

RBX technology enables the visualization of melanin and hemoglobin distributions in skin, the detection of hyper-pigmented and vascularized regions, and comparative analysis among patients. This level of skin analysis has not been possible with conventional skin imaging techniques. (Figure 5)
