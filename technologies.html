Technologies related to facial aesthetic analysis

This page briefly introduces three technologies used in the field of facial aesthetic analysis -- RBX Technology, 3D Viewer and Multiple Lighting Modalities. These technologies are used to capture and process facial images, which greatly assist in the analysis of facial skin conditions and the monitoring of changes in skin conditions during treatments.

RBX Technology

RBX Technology provides a more refined visualization of sub-surface melanin and vascular conditions for all skin types and ethnicities.

3D Viewer

A 3D viewer is used to visualize the surface of skin in three dimensions from any angle, rendered in natural skin tone, color relief, or gray scale.


Multiple Lighting Modalities

Multiple Lighting Modalities means that photograph patients using Standard 1, Blue excitation, Cross-polarized, Parallel-polarized, Standard 2, and Orange fluorescence modalities designed to enhance visualization of the skin features you wish to analyze.
